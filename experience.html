National Gallery of Art - Washington, DC

Summer Graduate Intern - Department of Gallery Archives

June - August 2019

In the summer of 2019 I had the fortunate opportunity to intern with the National Gallery of Art's Department of Gallery Archives. While there, I worked on a total of three projects. One of them involved scrapbooks that contain press clippings from 1937 to 1986. This collection contains over 300 volumes of scrapbooks of different materials (physically and content-wise). The most major issues with these scrapbooks were those related to preservation. To name a few, these scrapbooks had issues with warped pages, clippings that had come loose from their original pages, and adhesive residue and stains on the pages. In approaching this project, I conducted a report utilizing a sample of scrapbooks from the collection. After assessing the collection, I completed a rehousing demonstration in which I removed the contents of two scrapbooks and placed them in archival folders and boxes. This required placing the loose materials in plastic sleeves, and placing them behind their original pages in the folders. I also experimented with photocopying the pages in their original states (with the loose clippings in their original spots).

Another major project I participated in while with Gallery Archives was preparing materials for migration to NetX. This project was a collaboration with the Gallery's Division of Imaging and Visual Services to prepare metadata for exportation into the Gallery’s new DAM system. This collection totals around 50,000 objects, and explicit work involved the reconciliation of access and preservation versions of assets, the investigation of audio files, combining PDFs, and editing and correcting metadata in Excel. This also gave me the opportunity to write MARC records, as well as provide input into new software to be acquired by the Gallery Archives.

Third, I helped with the acquisition and inventory of the another department's files to the Archives. Tasks for this project included the physical transfer of the files from said department's offices, the investigation of each file for metadata and scope/content information, and then separating the files into three categories (retain, return, and missing). In the end, 10 cubic feet of files were retained by Gallery Archives, with less than 4 cubic feet being returned to the other department. This smaller group was then physically returned to the department's offices.

Along with these three major projects, I also helped Gallery Archives Staff with general tasks. These included pulling materials from G-76, participating in meetings between GA and other departments, as well as watching visiting researchers.
